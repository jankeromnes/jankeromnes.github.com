Why Open Source?

Friday, February 24, 2012

A lot of people ask me why I do Open Source. Don't I fear that my ideas get stolen, and used by some evil company to make money behind my back? The answer is simple: not at all. I actually encourage people to "steal" ideas from others, because that's how ideas spread, get refined and enriched. That's how the whole creative process functions, from the tiny spark of genious to the full-blown amazing new project. I would feel great honor if someone deemed my ideas worth putting into their own projects, and such personal satisfaction is far more precious to me than making big money on secrets, or forcing everybody to put my name everywhere.

The Open Source philosophy may seem a little idealistic, but in fact it's not. Open Source works, because it's a logical and very natural approach to doing things. When you want to go and make something cool because you just had an awesome idea, your very first thought is not (and should not be) how to protect this idea from the evil rest of the world so you can make money with it. Instead of isolating yourself and living in constant paranoia, you should share your views with the most people you can; this way you get feedback and valuable new input, and only then can you begin to see the shape of what you are going towards. Instead of working with a very few people you think you can trust, choose the whole World as a workforce for your projects.

This approach actually works for real companies like Mozilla who recently tripled their workforce; Google the internet giant behind the yearly Google Summer of Code, encouraging people around the globe to contribute to open source software projects; Red Hat going for the $1 billion barrier as the world's largest 100% open source business; and so many more, their numbers growing faster and faster.
