Modelling all known Space in 3D

Wednesday, February 08, 2012

As a huge space-enthusiast, I need to get a feeling of what's out there. I often read about space, watch new videos from NASA, ESA, SpaceX and Science Fiction series and movies. But this isn't enough. I want to be able to see and to explore things.

This is why I love Google Earth, an awesome project which is still largely unfinished. Exploring Earth is awesome. But not being able to zoom out, to detach from Earth and to wander around on your own is a huge limit. I'd like to go from Earth to the Moon, to Mars, to the Sun, to Alpha Centauri and back again. I'd love to see where Kepler 22b is located when I read about it. And I want to get a good idea of our position in the Solar System, in our Galaxy... where the Milky Way is situated in our Universe and where we're all headed.

This is why I started working on Sky Walker, a GPS-like Navigation System for Space. It uses real data from NASA and modern 3D web technologies to help you find your way through Space and Time. You can look at my source code or play with this little demo first.
